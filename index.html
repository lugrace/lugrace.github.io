About

I am a sophomore at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (Class of 2018). I'm interested in Computer Science, and have been learning it for the past 2 years. I typically use Java for my projects, but I am also proficient in CSS and HTML. I have also worked with Arduino to create a robot, amongst other simpler projects. Next year as a junior, I will be exploring Artifical Intelligence in Python. In my freetime throughout the next few months, I hope to learn about mobile app development and cybersecurity.

On the side, I'm passionate about Russian as the president of my school's Russian Club. I am also an active member of my community as an ambassador (volunteer) to the Smithsonian's National Museum of American History's Spark!Lab. There, I facilitate visitor experiences by welcoming and engaging visitors to discover the process of invention through hands-on stations and inquiry-based explorations. I am also interested in Computer Graphics, for which I primarily use Adobe Illustrator.
